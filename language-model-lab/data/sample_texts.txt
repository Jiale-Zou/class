In the early days of language modeling, researchers relied on simple counting methods.
An n-gram model estimates the probability of a word given a short history of previous words.
When the history becomes longer, the model can capture more local patterns, but it also faces data sparsity.
Smoothing methods such as Laplace smoothing reduce the impact of zero counts.

Neural language models learn distributed representations.
Recurrent neural networks can process sequences and predict the next symbol step by step.
Pretrained transformer models like BERT and GPT-2 provide strong baselines for many tasks.
